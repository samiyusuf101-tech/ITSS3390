What Makes a Great Starter Series?

5 min read • Beginner • Updated weekly

A great starter anime is easy to follow, emotionally engaging, and not overwhelming in length. Series like Demon Slayer, My Hero Academia, and Attack on Titan introduce viewers to anime’s strengths—expressive animation, dramatic battles, and relatable character arcs. They strike the perfect balance between action, humor, and meaningful storytelling.

From Manga to Screen: The Adaptation Pipeline

7 min read • Culture

Most anime begins as manga, where artists create entire worlds through black-and-white illustrations. Once a manga becomes popular, animators, storyboard artists, and voice actors collaborate to bring it to life. The goal is to stay faithful to the original art while elevating the story with motion, color, and sound.

Why Western Animators Borrow Anime Techniques

6 min read • Media

Anime’s influence on Western animation is huge. Shows like Avatar: The Last Airbender and Teen Titans borrow anime-inspired choreography, emotional expression, and visual pacing. This fusion of styles has shaped a new era of storytelling celebrated around the world.
